T-spins

In guideline-tetris games, T-spins are an important skill that players will have to learn as they progress. This is because taking t-spins will send the same amount of garbage lines to your opponent as a tetris does, making it a far more efficient method of attack. They can also easily be used to create spikes. This page will help you learn common stacking patterns you’ll be able to use in your games.

The Kaidan, or Stairs, T-Spin setup, discovered by Tetris DS player TCM-WC, is a prophecy T-Spin Double method that takes advantage of a step formation beside an empty column.

Imperial Cross

★★★☆☆

Imperial Cross (sometimes Imperial Crossing) is a family of T-Spin setups in games using SRS. It uses SRS's spin triple twist plus an additional 180 degree rotation to make a hurdle double, followed by a standard T-Spin double.

DT Cannon

★★★★☆

A setup where you can quickly send a TSD followed by a TST. First outlined by player TKI. DT Cannon is a very popular TSD → TST opener. In Tetris variants without instant lockdown it is best to hold the soft drop button until the T-Spin Double has been completed.
